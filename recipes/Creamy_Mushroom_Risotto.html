Creamy Mushroom Risotto

Description

A rich and comforting Italian classic featuring earthy wild mushrooms and a velvety Parmesan finish, perfect for a cozy evening meal.

Prep Time and Servings

Preparation Time:

25 minutes

Cook Time:

40 minutes

Total Time:

1 hour 5 minutes

Yield:

4 servings

Ingredients

(for 4 servings)

  • Grains & Liquids: 1 ½ cups Arborio rice, 5 ½ cups vegetable or chicken broth (warm), 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • Vegetables & Fungi: 300g mixed mushrooms (cremini, shiitake), 1 small onion (finely diced), 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • Pantry & Seasoning: 2 tablespoons butter, 2 tablespoons olive oil, ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese, ½ teaspoon salt, ¼ teaspoon black pepper, 1 tablespoon fresh thyme

Steps

  1. Prep the Ingredients: Clean and slice the mushrooms. Keep the broth warm in a separate pot over low heat.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In a large heavy-bottomed pot, heat the olive oil and 1 tablespoon of butter. Add the onion and cook until soft and translucent (about 5 minutes). Add garlic and mushrooms, cooking until the mushrooms release their moisture and brown slightly.
  3. Toast the Rice: Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 2 minutes until the edges become translucent and the grains are toasted.
  4. Deglaze (Alcohol-Free): Pour in the fresh lemon juice and a ladle of warm broth. Stir constantly until the liquid is fully absorbed. This adds the necessary acidity to balance the richness without using wine.
  5. Slow Cooking: Add the remaining warm broth one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until the liquid is almost fully absorbed before adding the next ladle. Continue this process for about 20-25 minutes until the rice is creamy but still al dente.
  6. Finish and Serve: Remove from heat. Stir in the remaining butter, Parmesan cheese, salt, pepper, and fresh thyme. Let it rest for 2 minutes before serving.
